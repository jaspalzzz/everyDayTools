How to use the California rate
California employers should not treat minimum wage as a generic national setting. This page tracks $16.50/hr for California, while Alaska, Arizona, Colorado may impose different amounts or timing. California is $9.25/hr above the federal floor, so the controlling rule depends on the worker's location and coverage.
- Use the state labor agency link before filing so the claim goes to the right office.
- For multi-state employers, verify the payroll profile was not copied from another state.
- Confirm the work location and whether a city or county ordinance applies.
- Check whether the employee is paid through a valid tipped-credit arrangement.

Who is covered by the California minimum wage
The California minimum wage covers most employees. Watch for these common exemptions:
- •Some trainees, learners, or workers in narrowly defined statutory programs
- •Family or domestic-service arrangements where a specific exemption applies
- •Certain agricultural or farm workers (federal FLSA exemptions may apply)
- •Tipped employees — a lower base rate may apply if tips close the gap
Being paid below minimum wage?
- 1. Calculate the shortfall: hours worked multiplied by the gap between the legal minimum and actual pay.
- 2. Keep records: pay stubs, time records, schedules, text messages, and rate notices.
- 3. Check whether a state, federal, tipped-credit, or local wage rule controls the claim.
- 4. File with the state labor agency or the US DOL Wage and Hour Division when informal correction fails.
Frequently asked questions
What is the minimum wage in California in 2026?
The minimum wage in California is $16.50/hr. $20/hr for fast food restaurant employees (AB 1228)
Does the federal minimum wage apply in California?
Federal law sets $7.25/hr, yet California's own minimum of $16.50/hr exceeds it. When a state rate beats the federal one, the FLSA makes the state rate controlling — so $16.50/hr is what applies in California.
What is the minimum wage for tipped employees in California?
Tipped employees in California may be paid a lower "tipped minimum wage" under federal or state law, provided tips bring total hourly earnings up to the full minimum wage. If tips do not make up the difference, the employer must pay the shortfall. The specific tipped credit rules in California are set by state law — check the California Department of Labor for the current tipped rate.
Are there local minimum wages higher than California's state rate?
In a few places, yes. Select cities and counties in California have adopted their own minimum wage above the state rate, and employers there owe the highest of the federal, state, or local figure. Your local government or https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/ can tell you if that applies to you.
What can I do if my employer pays me less than minimum wage in California?
If your employer pays below the minimum wage in California, you can: (1) file a wage complaint with the California Department of Labor; (2) file a federal complaint with the US Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division; or (3) bring a private civil lawsuit to recover unpaid wages. You may be entitled to back pay for up to 2–3 years plus additional damages. Retaliation for filing a wage complaint is illegal. Visit the California Department of Labor at https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/ to start a claim.
