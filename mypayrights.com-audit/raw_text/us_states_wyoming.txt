Frequently asked questions — Wyoming
Is PTO payout required by law in Wyoming?
It depends on your employer's written policy. Wyoming has no blanket state law requiring PTO payout, but if your employer's policy or employment contract provides for it, the payout is enforceable as wages. Accrued vacation must be paid unless a written forfeiture policy was provided and acknowledged.
How long does my employer have to pay my final paycheck in Wyoming?
In Wyoming, the deadlines differ based on how your employment ended. If you were terminated by your employer, your final paycheck is due within 5 business days. If you resigned voluntarily, the deadline is within 5 business days. If your employer misses these deadlines, you can file a wage claim with the Wyoming Department of Labor.
What is the minimum wage in Wyoming in 2025?
Wyoming's minimum wage is currently $7.25/hr (federal minimum). State minimum is $5.15/hr, but federal minimum of $7.25 applies to most employers That's the statewide floor — a handful of localities within Wyoming set their own higher minimum, so confirm the current figure with the Wyoming Department of Labor.
What can I do if my employer doesn't pay my final paycheck on time in Wyoming?
A late or missing final paycheck in Wyoming is grounds for a formal wage claim with the state's labor enforcement agency, and depending on the circumstances you may recover penalties or interest on top of the unpaid wages. A civil suit or a complaint to the federal Wage and Hour Division are also options if federal law covers your employer. Keep pay stubs, timesheets, and any written communication as evidence.
Does Wyoming require employers to include accrued sick leave in the final paycheck?
Usually no — sick leave and vacation are treated as different buckets under Wyoming PTO rules, unless your employer merges them into one combined PTO bank or your employment agreement says sick time vests as wages. Review your handbook to see which setup you have.
