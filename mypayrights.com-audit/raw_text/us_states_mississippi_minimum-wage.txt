How to use the Mississippi rate
Mississippi employers should not treat minimum wage as a generic national setting. This page tracks $7.25/hr (federal minimum) for Mississippi, while Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware may impose different amounts or timing. Mississippi follows the federal $7.25/hr floor unless a local or occupational rule changes the answer, so the controlling rule depends on the worker's location and coverage.
- Keep pay stubs, time records, schedules, and written rate-change notices together.
- Use the state labor agency link before filing so the claim goes to the right office.
- For multi-state employers, verify the payroll profile was not copied from another state.
- Confirm the work location and whether a city or county ordinance applies.

Who is covered by the Mississippi minimum wage
Coverage in Mississippi is broad — most employees qualify — though these groups are often exempt:
- •Independent contractors — minimum wage laws do not apply to genuine contractors
- •Some trainees, learners, or workers in narrowly defined statutory programs
- •Family or domestic-service arrangements where a specific exemption applies
- •Certain agricultural or farm workers (federal FLSA exemptions may apply)
Being paid below minimum wage?
- 1. Keep records: pay stubs, time records, schedules, text messages, and rate notices.
- 2. Check whether a state, federal, tipped-credit, or local wage rule controls the claim.
- 3. File with the state labor agency or the US DOL Wage and Hour Division when informal correction fails.
- 4. Ask whether back pay, liquidated damages, interest, or attorney-fee rules apply to the facts.
Frequently asked questions
Does the federal minimum wage apply in Mississippi?
Yes. Mississippi does not have a state minimum wage law that exceeds the federal rate. The federal minimum wage of $7.25/hr (set by the Fair Labor Standards Act) therefore applies. If the federal minimum wage is ever raised by Congress, that new rate would automatically apply in Mississippi.
What is the minimum wage for tipped employees in Mississippi?
Employers in Mississippi can apply a tip credit toward the minimum wage, meaning tipped staff can legally earn a lower direct wage as long as tips make up the rest. If they fall short, the employer owes the difference — the Mississippi Department of Labor lists the current rate.
Are there local minimum wages higher than Mississippi's state rate?
Possibly — some Mississippi localities set a higher minimum wage than the state floor, and where one applies, employers must pay whichever rate is highest among federal, state, and local. Confirm with your city or county, or via https://mdes.ms.gov, whether an ordinance applies to your workplace.
What can I do if my employer pays me less than minimum wage in Mississippi?
Being paid under the minimum wage in Mississippi gives you a few paths: a wage complaint with the Mississippi Department of Labor, a federal complaint with the US DOL's Wage and Hour Division, or a private civil suit. Back pay can often be recovered going back 2–3 years, plus additional damages in some cases — and it's illegal for your employer to retaliate for filing. Start a claim at https://mdes.ms.gov.
What is the minimum wage in Mississippi in 2026?
The minimum wage in Mississippi is $7.25/hr (federal minimum). Absent a state minimum wage law of its own, Mississippi falls back to the federal rate — currently $7.25/hr.
