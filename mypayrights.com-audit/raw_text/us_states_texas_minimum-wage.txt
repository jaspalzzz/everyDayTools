How to use the Texas rate
The safest way to use this Texas page is to separate three questions: the statewide or federal floor, any local rate, and any tipped-worker credit. The rate shown here is $7.25/hr (federal minimum); Texas follows the federal $7.25/hr floor unless a local or occupational rule changes the answer. - For multi-state employers, verify the payroll profile was not copied from another state.
- Confirm the work location and whether a city or county ordinance applies.
- Check whether the employee is paid through a valid tipped-credit arrangement.

Who is covered by the Texas minimum wage
The Texas minimum wage covers most employees. Watch for these common exemptions:
- •Some trainees, learners, or workers in narrowly defined statutory programs
- •Family or domestic-service arrangements where a specific exemption applies
- •Certain agricultural or farm workers (federal FLSA exemptions may apply)
- •Tipped employees — a lower base rate may apply if tips close the gap
Being paid below minimum wage?
- 1. Calculate the shortfall: hours worked multiplied by the gap between the legal minimum and actual pay.
- 2. Keep records: pay stubs, time records, schedules, text messages, and rate notices.
- 3. Check whether a state, federal, tipped-credit, or local wage rule controls the claim.
- 4. File with the state labor agency or the US DOL Wage and Hour Division when informal correction fails.
Frequently asked questions
What is the minimum wage in Texas in 2026?
The minimum wage in Texas is $7.25/hr (federal minimum). Because Texas has no state minimum wage law of its own, the federal floor of $7.25/hr is what employers must pay.
Does the federal minimum wage apply in Texas?
That's right. Texas has never enacted a state minimum wage above the federal Fair Labor Standards Act floor, so the $7.25/hr federal rate is what applies. A future federal increase would take effect in Texas without any state legislative action.
What is the minimum wage for tipped employees in Texas?
Employers in Texas can apply a tip credit toward the minimum wage, meaning tipped staff can legally earn a lower direct wage as long as tips make up the rest. If they fall short, the employer owes the difference — the Texas Department of Labor lists the current rate.
Are there local minimum wages higher than Texas's state rate?
In a few places, yes. Select cities and counties in Texas have adopted their own minimum wage above the state rate, and employers there owe the highest of the federal, state, or local figure. Your local government or https://www.twc.texas.gov/businesses/when-be-paid can tell you if that applies to you.
What can I do if my employer pays me less than minimum wage in Texas?
If your employer pays below the minimum wage in Texas, you can: (1) file a wage complaint with the Texas Department of Labor; (2) file a federal complaint with the US Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division; or (3) bring a private civil lawsuit to recover unpaid wages. You may be entitled to back pay for up to 2–3 years plus additional damages. Retaliation for filing a wage complaint is illegal. Visit the Texas Department of Labor at https://www.twc.texas.gov/businesses/when-be-paid to start a claim.
