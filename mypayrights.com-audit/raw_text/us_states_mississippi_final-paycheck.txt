How to audit a Mississippi final paycheck
For Mississippi, do not read the final-pay rule as a generic "next payroll" promise. The same deadline applies whether the worker was terminated or resigned: next regular payday. Workers should preserve the termination or resignation notice, the final timesheet, and any PTO policy before comparing the result with states like Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware.
- Written PTO or vacation policy in force on the separation date.
- Final pay stub, direct-deposit timestamp, or mailed-check postmark.
- Any written demand sent before filing a wage claim.
- Last day worked and the date employment officially ended.

What must be included in your final paycheck
These items must appear in a Mississippi final paycheck:
- ✓Any unreimbursed business expenses (where contractually required)
- ✓Earned bonus amounts that have vested under the written plan
- ✓Accrued PTO or vacation only when the state rule or employer policy requires payout
- ✓All earned wages through your last day (hourly or salary)
Whether accrued vacation or PTO must be included depends on Mississippi's PTO payout law. See Mississippi PTO payout rules →
If your employer pays late
- 1. File a wage claim with the state labor agency if payment still does not arrive.
- 2. Consider a civil lawsuit where penalties, interest, or attorney fees are available.
- 3. Keep the final pay stub and payment timestamp with the demand letter.
- 4. Document your last day worked and what you are owed.
Frequently asked questions
What must be included in my final paycheck in Mississippi?
Everything you earned through your last day belongs in the Mississippi final paycheck: base wages, overtime, and earned, calculable commissions or bonuses. Accrued vacation is governed by Mississippi's PTO payout law, not the final-pay rule itself. The employer cannot slip in unauthorized deductions.
What can I do if my employer is late with my final paycheck in Mississippi?
Missed the deadline in Mississippi? File a wage claim with the Mississippi Department of Labor at https://mdes.ms.gov, and consider a civil claim as well. Many cases recover unpaid wages plus penalties (and sometimes fees). Save documentation of your last day, what you were owed, and all employer communications.
Can my employer deduct money from my final paycheck in Mississippi?
Deductions from a Mississippi final paycheck are tightly limited to statutory ones (tax, garnishment) and anything you authorized in writing. Recovering the cost of a laptop, uniform, or advance by docking the check, without that consent or a court order, is unlawful.
Does Mississippi have penalties for employers who pay the final paycheck late?
Mississippi attaches real remedies to late final pay. On top of the unpaid amount you may be owed penalties, liquidated damages, or interest depending on the circumstances. File with the Mississippi Department of Labor at https://mdes.ms.gov, and a civil claim remains open to you.
How long does my employer have to give me my final paycheck in Mississippi?
Mississippi sets the final-pay deadline by how you left. A firing means the check is due next regular payday; quitting voluntarily means next regular payday. Employers may pay earlier, but paying later breaks the deadline.
