How to audit a Texas final paycheck
Texas's final-paycheck rule is a timing rule first and a documentation issue second. Terminations are due within 6 calendar days of discharge, while resignations are due next scheduled payday. That makes the separation date, the reason employment ended, and the next regular payday especially important. Nearby states such as Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware may use different triggers, so a regional payroll policy can be wrong for Texas.
- Final pay stub, direct-deposit timestamp, or mailed-check postmark.
- Any written demand sent before filing a wage claim.
- Last day worked and the date employment officially ended.
- Whether the separation was a resignation, layoff, discharge, or mutual end date.

What must be included in your final paycheck
A Texas final paycheck is only complete when it includes each of these:
- ✓Earned bonus amounts that have vested under the written plan
- ✓Accrued PTO or vacation only when the state rule or employer policy requires payout
- ✓All earned wages through your last day (hourly or salary)
- ✓Overtime pay owed for hours worked
Whether accrued vacation or PTO must be included depends on Texas's PTO payout law. See Texas PTO payout rules →
If your employer pays late
- 1. Consider a civil lawsuit where penalties, interest, or attorney fees are available.
- 2. Keep the final pay stub and payment timestamp with the demand letter.
- 3. Document your last day worked and what you are owed.
- 4. Confirm whether the deadline changed because you resigned rather than being terminated.
Frequently asked questions
How long does my employer have to give me my final paycheck in Texas?
In Texas, the deadline depends on how your employment ended. If your employer terminated you, your final paycheck is due within 6 calendar days of discharge. If you resigned voluntarily, it is due next scheduled payday. These are minimum legal deadlines — your employer can pay sooner, but not later.
What must be included in my final paycheck in Texas?
A lawful Texas final paycheck includes all wages through your last day worked, any overtime, and commissions/bonuses that have vested and can be calculated. Whether unused PTO is added depends on Texas's PTO payout law. Only legally authorized deductions (tax, garnishments) are permitted.
What can I do if my employer is late with my final paycheck in Texas?
Missed the deadline in Texas? File a wage claim with the Texas Department of Labor at https://www.twc.texas.gov/businesses/when-be-paid, and consider a civil claim as well. Many cases recover unpaid wages plus penalties (and sometimes fees). Save documentation of your last day, what you were owed, and all employer communications.
Can my employer deduct money from my final paycheck in Texas?
In Texas, a final paycheck can only be reduced by legally mandated amounts (tax, garnishment) or deductions you signed off on in writing. Docking pay to recover company property or an advance, absent that authorization or a court process, violates wage law.
Does Texas have penalties for employers who pay the final paycheck late?
Yes — late final pay carries remedies in Texas. You may recover the wages plus waiting-time penalties, liquidated damages, or interest, depending on the facts. The Texas labor agency can investigate and order payment, and you can also sue in state court. Start a claim at https://www.twc.texas.gov/businesses/when-be-paid.
