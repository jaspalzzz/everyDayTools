What makes Texas different
Texas has no state minimum wage above the federal floor and no state income tax, which changes the calculus for both employers and workers compared to states like California or New York. Wage claims are handled under the Texas Payday Law (Labor Code Chapter 61) through the Texas Workforce Commission rather than a dedicated labor department, and a claim generally must be filed within 180 days of the date wages were due — a materially shorter window than several neighboring states allow.

Frequently asked questions — Texas
Is PTO payout required by law in Texas?
Not by state mandate. Texas leaves PTO payout entirely up to the employer — there's no law forcing a cash-out of unused vacation when employment ends, so check your handbook. No statute requires payout; entirely policy-driven.
How long does my employer have to pay my final paycheck in Texas?
Texas draws a line between firings and resignations for final-pay timing. Fired employees must be paid within 6 calendar days of discharge; those who resign are owed their pay next scheduled payday. A wage claim with the Texas Department of Labor is the remedy if either deadline slips.
What is the minimum wage in Texas in 2025?
As of 2026, Texas's minimum wage sits at $7.25/hr (federal minimum). Local ordinances in some Texas cities or counties can set a higher floor, so it's worth checking with the Texas Department of Labor for the exact rate that applies where you work.
What can I do if my employer doesn't pay my final paycheck on time in Texas?
A late or missing final paycheck in Texas is grounds for a formal wage claim with the state's labor enforcement agency, and depending on the circumstances you may recover penalties or interest on top of the unpaid wages. A civil suit or a complaint to the federal Wage and Hour Division are also options if federal law covers your employer. Keep pay stubs, timesheets, and any written communication as evidence.
Does Texas require employers to include accrued sick leave in the final paycheck?
Not typically. Texas's PTO payout requirements center on vacation time, and sick leave is its own category unless your employer combines them into a single PTO bank or your agreement vests sick time as earned wages. Your handbook or offer letter will settle which rule applies to you.
