How to use the Kansas rate
For Kansas, the practical question is which wage floor controls the actual workplace. The page rate is $7.25/hr (federal minimum), and Kansas follows the federal $7.25/hr floor unless a local or occupational rule changes the answer. In the Midwest region, compare that with Illinois, Indiana, Iowa before assuming a multi-state employer uses one uniform rate.
- Confirm the work location and whether a city or county ordinance applies.
- Check whether the employee is paid through a valid tipped-credit arrangement.
- Compare gross hourly pay before overtime premiums against the controlling minimum.
- Keep pay stubs, time records, schedules, and written rate-change notices together.

Who is covered by the Kansas minimum wage
The minimum wage applies to most employees in Kansas. Common exemptions include:
- •Certain agricultural or farm workers (federal FLSA exemptions may apply)
- •Tipped employees — a lower base rate may apply if tips close the gap
- •Student workers at certain educational institutions (may qualify for a subminimum rate)
- •Independent contractors — minimum wage laws do not apply to genuine contractors
Being paid below minimum wage?
- 1. Calculate the shortfall: hours worked multiplied by the gap between the legal minimum and actual pay.
- 2. Keep records: pay stubs, time records, schedules, text messages, and rate notices.
- 3. Check whether a state, federal, tipped-credit, or local wage rule controls the claim.
- 4. File with the state labor agency or the US DOL Wage and Hour Division when informal correction fails.
Frequently asked questions
What is the minimum wage in Kansas in 2026?
The minimum wage in Kansas is $7.25/hr (federal minimum). Because Kansas has no state minimum wage law of its own, the federal floor of $7.25/hr is what employers must pay.
Does the federal minimum wage apply in Kansas?
Yes. Unlike states that have set their own higher minimum wage, Kansas relies entirely on the federal rate of $7.25/hr under the Fair Labor Standards Act. Any change to that federal figure — up or down — would take effect in Kansas immediately, since there is no state-level floor to fall back on.
What is the minimum wage for tipped employees in Kansas?
Kansas lets employers pay a reduced base rate to tipped staff, provided the combination of tips and base pay reaches the full minimum wage — the employer must cover any gap. Check https://www.dol.ks.gov for the exact tipped-wage figure currently in effect.
Are there local minimum wages higher than Kansas's state rate?
Some cities and counties in Kansas may have enacted local minimum wages higher than the state rate. Where a local ordinance applies, employers must pay the highest of federal, state, or local rates. Check with your local city or county government, or https://www.dol.ks.gov, for any applicable local minimum wage that may apply to your job.
What can I do if my employer pays me less than minimum wage in Kansas?
If your employer pays below the minimum wage in Kansas, you can: (1) file a wage complaint with the Kansas Department of Labor; (2) file a federal complaint with the US Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division; or (3) bring a private civil lawsuit to recover unpaid wages. You may be entitled to back pay for up to 2–3 years plus additional damages. Retaliation for filing a wage complaint is illegal. Visit the Kansas Department of Labor at https://www.dol.ks.gov to start a claim.
