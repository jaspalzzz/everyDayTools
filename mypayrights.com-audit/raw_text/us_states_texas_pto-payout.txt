What this means in practice
When reviewing a PTO payout in Texas, separate the issue into accrual, policy, and payment timing. Each one needs its own document trail.
In Texas, the strongest claim usually comes from the employer's own written policy. Save the handbook and paystub showing accrued PTO, then compare the policy language with your final paycheck.
How to estimate the payout
Use this formula: unused PTO hours x final hourly rate. For salaried employees, convert annual salary into an hourly or daily equivalent first. The result is gross pay before federal, state, and payroll tax withholding.

State-specific checkpoints
In Texas, a final paycheck — including any PTO payout that is owed — is due within 6 calendar days of discharge when the employer ends the job and next scheduled payday when you resign. Confirm the current rule against the Texas labor agency before you file, since deadlines and payout rules can change between legislative sessions.
Texas's timing rule is not one-size-fits-all, so the separation reason should be part of the wage-claim file.

Common questions
Does Texas require PTO payout when I leave?
For Texas workers, state law does not create a default vacation cash-out right. A payout generally comes from policy, contract, or company practice. No statute requires payout; entirely policy-driven.
Where do I file a PTO payout claim in Texas?
Start with the Texas labor agency: https://www.twc.texas.gov/businesses/when-be-paid. Include your final paystub, PTO balance, handbook policy, resignation or termination date, and any payroll messages about unused vacation.
When should unused PTO be paid in Texas?
The timing question in Texas follows final-pay rules once PTO is owed. That means within 6 calendar days of discharge for a firing and next scheduled payday for a voluntary quit.
Can employers in Texas use a "use it or lose it" policy?
Texas employers often have room to set forfeiture rules, but employees should check whether the handbook actually says unused vacation is lost at separation.
How do I calculate unused PTO value in Texas?
For a payout estimate in Texas, multiply the unused hours on your PTO ledger by your final regular hourly rate. Salaried workers can convert annual salary into an hourly or daily rate first.
