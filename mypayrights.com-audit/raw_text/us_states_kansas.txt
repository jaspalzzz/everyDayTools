Frequently asked questions — Kansas
Is PTO payout required by law in Kansas?
No state requirement exists in Kansas. Employers here are free to set their own use-it-or-lose-it or payout policy, and that written policy — not a statute — determines what you're owed. No payout mandate; policy-driven.
How long does my employer have to pay my final paycheck in Kansas?
Kansas sets separate deadlines depending on why you left. A termination means your final paycheck is due next regular payday, while a resignation gives your employer until next regular payday. Missing either deadline is grounds for a wage claim with the Kansas Department of Labor.
What is the minimum wage in Kansas in 2025?
Kansas pegs its statewide minimum wage at $7.25/hr (federal minimum). Local governments in Kansas can set a higher rate, so double-check against the Kansas Department of Labor before assuming the statewide figure applies to you.
What can I do if my employer doesn't pay my final paycheck on time in Kansas?
Start by filing a wage claim with Kansas's labor enforcement agency if your final paycheck is late — state law may entitle you to the unpaid wages plus penalties or interest. Federally covered employers can also be reported to the U.S. Department of Labor's Wage and Hour Division, or you can pursue a civil claim. Keep a paper trail: hours worked, pay stubs, and any messages with your employer.
Does Kansas require employers to include accrued sick leave in the final paycheck?
In most cases, no. PTO payout rules in Kansas apply primarily to accrued vacation or PTO. Sick leave is typically treated separately — unless your employer has a combined PTO bank or the sick leave has vested as wages under your employment agreement. Check your offer letter and employee handbook for the specific terms of your employer's sick leave policy.
